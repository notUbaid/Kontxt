# Welcome

You're about to build a marketplace, solo, as a personal project. Not a hackathon sprint, not a funded startup — something you'll actually finish, understand end to end, and could be proud to put in front of a hiring manager or just use yourself.

This module sets expectations before you write a line of code. Skipping it usually means re-learning these lessons the expensive way, three weeks in.

---

## What "Personal Project" Mode Actually Optimizes For

Every recommendation across this track is filtered through four priorities, in this order:

1. **You finish it.** A marketplace with three core features that works beats one with fifteen that doesn't.
2. **You understand every part of it.** If you can't explain a piece of your own app, that piece is a liability, not an asset.
3. **It costs close to nothing to run.** Solo projects die from $40/month bills nobody's using, more often than from bad code.
4. **It's maintainable by one person.** No infrastructure that needs a team to operate.

> **Decision:** Anything that conflicts with these four — a trendy tool, a "best practice" built for teams of twenty, a feature that impresses nobody but took a week — gets cut. This track will tell you explicitly when something's being cut and why.

This is different from Hackathon mode, which optimizes for a 3-minute demo, and different from Production mode, which optimizes for scale and a team. Personal mode optimizes for *you, finishing, and learning.*

## What You're Actually Building

A marketplace connects two sides — people who supply something and people who want it — and makes the exchange between them work. That's it at the core. Everything else (search, payments, messaging, reviews) exists to make that exchange trustworthy and easy.

Before this track is done, you'll have made real decisions about:

- What's actually being exchanged, and between whom
- How you'll solve the "empty marketplace" problem every new marketplace starts with
- How money or value actually moves between people
- How strangers trust each other enough to transact

These aren't implementation details. They're the actual hard problems of building a marketplace — the code is usually the easy part once these are decided well.

## How This Track Is Structured

```
Phase 0 — Discovery        → what you're building, and why it'll work
Phase 1 — Product Design   → what it looks like, how it feels to use
Phase 2 — Architecture     → how it's built, before you build it
Phase 3 — Development      → actually building it
Phase 4 — Production       → making it reliable enough to trust
Phase 5 — Launch           → getting it in front of real people
Phase 6 — Growth           → what happens after people start using it
```

Each phase builds on decisions from the last one. Discovery decisions shape your architecture. Architecture shapes what you can build in Phase 3 without rewriting it. Skipping ahead usually means redoing earlier work once you hit a wall a later phase would have warned you about.

> **Tip:** You don't have to complete every topic in every phase before moving on, but don't skip Phase 0 entirely. It's the cheapest phase to do well and the most expensive to skip.

## How AI Fits Into This Track

You'll use AI tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, or similar) constantly throughout this build. That's expected — most modules include ready-to-use prompts for exactly that.

What this track will keep reinforcing: **AI writes the code, you make the decisions.** A prompt that says "build me a marketplace" produces something generic. A prompt that says "build me a listing schema for a marketplace where sellers offer hourly tutoring sessions, where availability matters more than inventory count" produces something useful — because you did the thinking first.

Every module that includes a prompt also includes how to check what comes back. AI output that looks confident and correct is not the same as AI output that is correct. You'll learn to spot the difference as you go, not just be told to "always verify."

## What Success Looks Like Here

Not: a marketplace with every feature a list like Etsy or Airbnb has.

Instead: a marketplace where you can explain *why* every major decision was made, that runs reliably for the niche you picked, and that you built (with AI's help) rather than copy-pasted into existence.

If you finish this track with a working app and you can't explain why you chose your tech stack, how your data model represents a transaction, or how a stranger trusts a seller on your platform — that's a signal to slow down, not a signal you're behind.

## Before You Start: A Few Honest Notes

- **This will take longer than a weekend.** Personal mode assumes evenings and weekends over weeks, not a hackathon sprint. Pace accordingly.
- **You will change your mind about scope.** That's normal. Phase 0 exists to make that changing-your-mind happen on paper, not three weeks into building the wrong thing.
- **"Solo developer" doesn't mean "no standards."** It means standards scoped to what one person can realistically maintain — not no standards at all.
