# Submission Checklist

This is the final pass before you share this project — whether that's submitting it somewhere, sending it to friends, publishing it publicly, or simply calling it "done" for this stage. Everything you've built across every phase gets one last verification, together, as a complete system.

## The Decision You're Actually Making

Not "is the app finished." It's: **if someone I've never met opens this right now, with zero context from me, does the whole thing — app, story, legal, trust — hold together?**

This is the same discipline as the Launch Checklist from Phase 5, but applied to the *presentation* of the project, not just its technical function.

## The Full-System Check

- Live marketplace URL works, checked in an incognito window
- A real seller listing and real buyer path both function end to end
- Legal documents (Terms, Privacy Policy) are linked and accurate, matching what the app actually does now
- Refund/dispute policy still matches the actual payment integration behavior
- Pitch deck file opens correctly in whatever format you'll actually present it in

> **️ Warning:** It's common for legal documents and policies written earlier in the project to drift slightly from what the app now actually does, especially after roadmap changes from this phase. Read your Terms of Service and Refund Policy fresh, one more time, against the current live app — not the app as it existed when you first wrote them.

## Story and Materials Check

- Deck leads with the coordination/trust problem, not a feature list
- At least one real number (liquidity, sell-through, transaction count) is accurate and current
- Demo script has been rehearsed against the live, deployed app — not localhost
- A fallback (backup listing or recording) is ready in case the live demo doesn't cooperate

> ** Best Practice:** Do one final full run-through as if you were a stranger — deck, then live demo, then a moment of "what would someone ask me right after this" — the day before you actually present or submit, not weeks in advance when things may have since changed.

## Trust & Safety Final Pass

- Moderation/reporting mechanism is functional, even if lightly used so far
- Payment processor account is fully verified and able to process real transactions
- You have a way to be notified if something goes wrong after you share this more widely

> **️ Warning:** Sharing your marketplace more broadly (a submission, a public post, a wider friend group) often brings a spike in new users all at once. Confirm your notification and moderation systems from earlier phases can handle that spike before it happens, not after you discover a gap live.

## Use AI for One Final Cold Read

**Prompt — Final Submission Readiness Review**
```
I'm about to share/submit my personal marketplace project. Here's a 
summary of the app, the legal documents in place, and my pitch materials:
[paste summary: what the app does, current liquidity signal, legal 
docs status, deck outline]

Act as someone seeing this for the first time. Identify:
1. Any inconsistency between what the deck/story claims and what the 
   legal documents or app actually reflect
2. Anything that looks unfinished or would raise an immediate question
3. The single most likely thing to go wrong if I get a sudden spike 
   in new users right after sharing this
```

> ** Token Efficiency:** This is the natural final check for the whole project — run it once, as a genuine last gate, rather than repeatedly through development. It's most valuable when everything else is already finished and you need a final, holistic sanity check.

## Validate, For Real, One Last Time

- Full buyer-seller loop tested end to end on the live app, today, not from memory of an earlier test
- Legal and policy documents match current app behavior
- Deck and demo have been rehearsed together as one presentation, not separately
- You have a plan for what happens if this gets more attention than expected

## Common Mistakes

- Presenting a deck with claims that no longer match the current state of the app
- Legal documents that drifted out of sync with roadmap changes made during this phase
- No rehearsal of deck and demo together as one continuous flow
- No plan for a sudden usage spike right after sharing more widely
- Treating this as a formality instead of a genuine final verification pass

## Quick Reference

| Verify one final time | Rehearse together | Prepare for |
|---|---|---|
| Live app end-to-end, as a stranger would use it | Deck + demo, back to back | A possible spike in new users |
| Legal docs match current app behavior | Story + real numbers cited | Questions about liquidity or trust mechanisms |
| Notification/moderation systems are functional | Fallback plan for the demo | Being asked what's next on the roadmap |

## You're Done

From discovery and cold-start strategy through architecture, legal grounding, and a full growth cycle, this marketplace has been built the way an experienced team would approach it — grounded in real evidence at every stage, not just feature-by-feature guesswork. That discipline, more than any single feature, is what separates a marketplace that actually works from one that only looks like it does.
