# Analytics (Data & Attribution)

## The Difference Between Vanity Metrics and Revenue

In a personal project, installing Google Analytics to watch page views is sufficient. In a production marketplace, analytics is the nervous system of your business. If you do not know your exact Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) compared to your Lifetime Value (LTV), you will burn through your capital and fail.

A production analytics stack must track deep funnel conversion, marketing attribution, and platform leakage—all while strictly complying with GDPR and CCPA privacy laws.

---

## The Two-Tier Analytics Stack

Do not attempt to use one tool for everything. Production environments separate behavioral product data from immutable financial data.

| Analytics Tier | Purpose | Enterprise Tooling |
|---|---|---|
| **Product Analytics** | User behavior, funnel drop-off, A/B testing | PostHog, Mixpanel, Amplitude |
| **Business Intelligence (BI)** | Exact revenue, fee splits, dispute rates, LTV | Postgres Read-Replica, BigQuery, Snowflake, Metabase |

> [!DECISION]
> Default to **PostHog** for Product Analytics. It combines event tracking, session replay, and feature flags into a single open-source platform, making it the current standard for modern marketplaces.

---

## Client-Side vs. Server-Side Tracking

If you rely entirely on a JavaScript snippet on your frontend to track purchases, you will lose up to 30% of your data to Ad Blockers (e.g., uBlock Origin) and Apple's ITP (Intelligent Tracking Prevention).

**The Production Rule:**
* **Client-Side:** Use frontend tracking for low-stakes behavioral events (`listing_viewed`, `search_performed`, `filter_applied`).
* **Server-Side:** Use backend tracking for critical business events (`checkout_completed`, `listing_published`, `user_banned`). Fire the event to PostHog/Mixpanel directly from your Node.js/Next.js backend.

---

## Attribution (UTM Parameters)

To scale the demand side of your marketplace, you will eventually run ads or sponsor influencers. You must know which channels actually generate revenue, not just clicks.

When a user lands on `yourmarketplace.com?utm_source=facebook&utm_campaign=summer_sale`:
1. The frontend must capture those UTM parameters and store them in a secure `HttpOnly` cookie or pass them to the backend during signup.
2. The `Users` table should contain a `utm_source` column.
3. Now, you can run a BI query: "What is the total revenue generated by users acquired from the 'summer_sale' campaign?"

---

## GDPR, CCPA, and PII Anonymization

Marketplaces handle highly sensitive data. You face massive fines if you send Personally Identifiable Information (PII) to third-party analytics tools without consent.

**Strict Privacy Rules:**
- **Anonymization:** Never send an email address, plain-text name, or physical address to PostHog or Mixpanel. Send an obfuscated `user_id` or a hashed email.
- **Cookie Consent:** In the EU and California, you cannot fire tracking pixels until the user explicitly clicks "Accept Analytics Cookies." Your architecture must block the initialization of frontend tracking libraries until this boolean is true.
- **Message Privacy:** Never log the content of user-to-user messages into your analytics tool. Only log the metadata (`message_sent`, `thread_id`).

## Do's and Don'ts of Production Analytics

- **DO track the "Zero Search Results" metric.** The most critical metric for marketplace liquidity is how often buyers search for something and find nothing. Track exactly what keywords return zero results so you know what supply to recruit.
- **DON'T build custom dashboards early.** Connect Metabase or Superset directly to a Read-Replica of your Postgres database. Writing custom SQL queries in Metabase is infinitely faster than building a custom React admin dashboard with Chart.js.
- **DO track Time-to-First-Action.** Measure the time between a seller creating an account and publishing their first listing. If this metric increases, your onboarding flow is broken.
- **DON'T track every single click.** Event bloat makes your analytics unusable. Track core funnel transitions, not arbitrary button hovers.

````prompt
I am using Next.js (App Router). Write an Edge Middleware function that intercepts incoming requests, looks for `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, and `utm_campaign` in the URL search parameters, and securely stores them in an `HttpOnly` cookie that lasts for 30 days, so the backend can read them during the user signup flow.
````

## Validating What AI Generates

- **Check for PII leaks:** If the AI writes a tracking payload that includes `user.email` or `user.phone`, reject it entirely and demand it be replaced with a hashed identifier or UUID.
- **Verify Server-Side Reliability:** Ensure backend event tracking calls are wrapped in `try/catch` blocks or dispatched via a queue so that if PostHog's API is down, it does not crash your main checkout flow.
