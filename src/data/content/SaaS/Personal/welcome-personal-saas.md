# Welcome to Kontxt

 **Estimated Time:** 5 Minutes

---

You have an idea for a SaaS product.

Maybe it's been sitting in your notes for months. Maybe it came to you last week. Maybe you've already tried to build it once and stopped.

Whatever brought you here — this is where you start building it properly.

---

## This Mode Is Different

Hackathon mode optimizes for 48 hours and a demo.

Personal mode optimizes for something harder: building a product you can actually ship, maintain, and grow — as one person, with limited time, using AI as your engineering partner.

That means different tradeoffs. Different priorities. A different pace.

You won't be cutting corners to hit a demo deadline. You'll be making deliberate decisions so you don't rebuild everything six months from now.

---

## What Kontxt Does

Kontxt guides you through one engineering decision at a time.

Not all at once. Not in the abstract. At the moment you actually need to make it.

Each module gives you:
- The context to understand why a decision matters
- The criteria to make it well for your specific project
- The AI prompts to execute it without starting from scratch
- The validation to know when you're done

You won't finish a module with more questions than you started with.

---

## What You're Building Toward

By the end of this journey, you'll have:

- A real product — not a prototype, not a tutorial clone
- A codebase you understand, not one you copy-pasted
- Architecture decisions you can defend
- The ability to work with AI like an engineer, not a prompt-guesser

This is not a course. There are no lectures. There are no certificates.

There is only your product — and whether it ships.

---

## How Personal Mode Works

You'll move through five phases:

| Phase | Focus | Goal |
|-------|-------|------|
| **0 — Discovery** | Personas, idea, features | Know exactly what you're building and for whom |
| **1 — Product** | PRD, design, flows | Decide what the product does before writing code |
| **2 — Architecture** | Stack, auth, database, APIs | Make the foundational decisions that shape everything else |
| **3 — Development** | Backend, frontend, emails, analytics | Build the actual product with AI as your pair programmer |
| **4 — Production Lite** | Performance, backups, deployment, security | Ship something that won't embarrass you |
| **5 — Growth Lite** | Feedback, roadmap, SEO, presentation | Learn from users and keep improving |

Move at your own pace. Come back whenever you're ready for the next decision.

## A Word on AI

You already know how to get AI to write code.

What Kontxt teaches is something different: how to use AI the way senior engineers do.

That means knowing:
- What to ask for and what to specify
- When to trust the output and when to push back
- How to validate what AI generates
- What context AI actually needs to do good work
- When to start a fresh conversation versus continuing

AI will write a lot of your code. That's fine. Knowing whether that code is good — that's the skill.

## Before You Start

One question worth sitting with before Phase 0:

> *"Am I building this because I want to build it — or because I want it to exist?"*

Both are valid reasons. But they lead to different decisions.

If you want to build it — you'll enjoy the process even if few people use it.

If you want it to exist — you'll need to think seriously about who else needs it, and whether they'll pay for it.

Knowing which one you are will shape every decision from here.
