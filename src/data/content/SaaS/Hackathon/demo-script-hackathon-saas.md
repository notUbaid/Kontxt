# Demo Script

🕒 **Estimated Time:** 15 Minutes

---

A demo script is not what you say.

It is the exact sequence of clicks, words, and transitions you will perform — rehearsed until it feels natural but never improvised.

The difference between a demo that wins and a demo that loses is almost never the product. It's the script.

---

## Why You Need a Script

Under pressure, your brain defaults to narrating what judges can already see.

> *"So here you can see the dashboard... and if I click here... it loads the... okay so this is the main page..."*

A script eliminates this. You've already decided what every moment means before you're standing in front of judges.

A script also forces you to find dead moments — clicks that take too long, transitions that feel awkward, features that don't read clearly live. Fix those in rehearsal, not during the demo.

---

## The Anatomy of a Demo Script

Every demo has the same four parts regardless of what you built:

```
1. SETUP      — Context. Who is the user, what situation are they in.
2. ACTION     — The core thing your product does. The moment of value.
3. RESULT     — What changed. What the user now has that they didn't before.
4. SIGNAL     — One line that anchors the judge's takeaway.
```

Your entire demo is just this loop, run once or twice through your product's core flow.

---

## Format: Click-by-Click Script

Write your script as a two-column document.

Left column: what you click. Right column: what you say.

| SCREEN / ACTION | WORDS |
|-----------------|-------|
| Open app, already logged in as Sarah Chen | "This is Sarah. She runs a 6-person agency and manages 40+ active clients." |
| Show dashboard — revenue chart trending up, 3 alerts | "The moment she opens the platform, she can see her pipeline at a glance. Three deals need attention today." |
| Click first alert — overdue invoice | "This invoice has been outstanding for 14 days. Normally she'd write a follow-up email herself." |
| Click "Auto Follow-up" button | "She clicks one button." |
| Show generated email preview | "Our engine drafts a personalized follow-up based on the client's history, invoice amount, and how overdue it is." |
| Click Send | "Sent. Logged. Done in four seconds." |
| Return to dashboard — show activity feed updated | "Every action is tracked. Her whole team sees it. Nothing falls through the cracks." |

This format forces you to choreograph the demo before you're live.

---

## Script Writing Rules

**Lead with the user, not the product**
Don't say *"our app has a dashboard."* Say *"Sarah opens the platform and immediately knows what needs her attention."* Judges should see a person being helped, not a product being shown.

**Name the moment of value explicitly**
There is one moment in your demo where the product does the thing it exists to do. Script a pause there. Let it land. Don't rush past it.

**Say less than you think you should**
Every word you say while clicking is competing with what judges are reading on screen. Trust the product. Silence is fine.

**Never narrate what's visible**
If the button says "Send Invoice" and you click it — don't say "I'm clicking Send Invoice." Say what it means: *"Invoice is out. Client gets it instantly."*

**Script your transitions**
The weakest moment of most demos is moving between features. Script the transition line:
> *"That's the core flow. One more thing worth showing — how the team view works."*

Without that line, you'll stumble. With it, you sound in control.

## The Golden Path (Defined)

Your demo should follow exactly one path through your product.

```
Entry point (login or landing)
  ↓
Dashboard or home state (show the data is real)
  ↓
Core action (the thing your product uniquely does)
  ↓
Result (what changed, what the user now has)
  ↓
[Optional] One supporting feature that adds credibility
  ↓
Exit / return to slides
```

Do not deviate from this path during the live demo.

> **Warning**
>
> Do not say "let me show you one more thing" unless it's scripted.
> Unscripted additions run over time, hit edge cases, and lose judges mid-flow.
> Every "one more thing" should have been decided in advance.

---

## Timing Your Demo

For a 5-minute pitch, your demo should be **2 to 2.5 minutes maximum.**

| Segment | Time |
|---------|------|
| Setup — context + user intro | 15–20 sec |
| Core action + result | 60–90 sec |
| Supporting feature (if included) | 20–30 sec |
| Exit line back to slides | 5–10 sec |

Run a timer during rehearsal. If you're consistently hitting 3+ minutes on the demo, cut a feature.

## Handling Live Failures

Something will go wrong. Script your recovery too.

| What fails | What you say |
|-----------|-------------|
| Page loads slowly | "While that loads — this is the moment where the system is running [X] in the background." |
| Feature breaks | "I'll show you this in our backup recording — but you get the idea of the flow." |
| Wrong account open | "Let me switch to our demo account — " [switch quickly, don't apologize] |
| Forgot what comes next | Pause. Take a breath. Say: "And here's where it gets interesting." [buy 3 seconds] |

> **Tip**
>
> Rehearse your failure recoveries out loud. They feel unnatural until they don't.
> The judge won't remember the failure. They'll remember how you handled it.

---

## The Exit Line

Every demo needs a clean exit back to slides.

Write this line exactly. Rehearse it exactly.

> *"That's the core flow. In under two minutes, Sarah went from chasing a late payment to having it handled automatically. Let me show you how we built this."*

This line does three things: summarizes the value, references your demo user by name, and transitions to the next slide naturally.

---

## Rehearsal Protocol

- [ ] Read the script aloud — full speed, with the app open
- [ ] Run the full demo twice without stopping
- [ ] Time it — cut anything that pushes past 2.5 minutes
- [ ] Run it once with a teammate watching — get their honest reaction
- [ ] Run it once while someone asks you a question mid-demo (practice ignoring it)
- [ ] Run it the morning of with demo data fresh and golden-path account loaded

> **Warning**
>
> Do not rehearse by reading the script silently.
> Your hands and your mouth need to be in sync. Only live rehearsal builds that.

---

## Demo Script Checklist

- [ ] Script written in two-column click-by-click format
- [ ] Golden-path flow decided — no deviations planned
- [ ] Moment of value identified and scripted with a pause
- [ ] Transitions between sections scripted
- [ ] No narration of visible UI elements
- [ ] Timed at under 2.5 minutes
- [ ] Exit line written and memorized
- [ ] Failure recoveries rehearsed out loud
- [ ] Demo data loaded and verified on presentation device
- [ ] Full rehearsal done at least twice with the app live
