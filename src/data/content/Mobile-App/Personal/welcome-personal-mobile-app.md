# Welcome to Kontxt

You want to build a mobile app.

Not a hackathon project. Not a quick demo. A real app — something you're proud to ship, that works reliably, that you can maintain and grow over time.

That is a different kind of build. And it requires a different kind of thinking.

Kontxt is here to guide you through it.

---

## What This Track Is

This is the **Personal Mobile App** track.

It is designed for solo builders and small teams who are building for real users — not for a competition deadline, not to impress investors in 48 hours, but to ship something that lasts.

The emphasis here is on:

- **Learning while building** — understanding why decisions matter, not just what to do
- **Simplicity over cleverness** — choosing the right tool, not the most impressive one
- **Maintainability** — writing code you can return to in three months and still understand
- **Sustainable pace** — building something you can finish, not just start

You are not racing. You are building.

---

## What You Will Build

By the end of this track, you will have:

- A clearly defined idea with a real user in mind
- A product requirements document that guides every decision
- A design system and navigation architecture
- A full-stack mobile app with auth, a database, and backend logic
- An app that handles offline gracefully
- A production deployment on the Play Store and/or App Store
- Analytics, a feedback loop, and a roadmap for what's next

That is a complete software product — built by you, with AI as your collaborator.

---

## How Kontxt Works

Each module covers one engineering decision or skill.

Inside each module you will find:

- **Concepts** — the mental models experienced engineers use
- **Decisions** — the choices you need to make, and how to make them well
- **Prompts** — ready-to-use AI prompts that produce professional outputs
- **Validation** — how to review AI-generated work the way an engineer would
- **Checklists** — clear gates before you move to the next step

You work through modules in order. Each one builds on the last.

You do not need to memorise everything. You need to understand enough to make good decisions and recognise when something is wrong.

---

## The Role of AI in This Track

AI will write a significant portion of your code.

That is not a shortcut. That is modern software development.

But AI does not make engineering decisions. It does not know your users, your constraints, or your priorities. It does not know when a schema is wrong for your data, when a library is overkill, or when a security decision will cost you later.

You make those decisions. Kontxt teaches you how.

The most important skill in this track is not prompting. It is **knowing what to ask for and how to evaluate what you get.**

## Before You Start

A few things to have in place:

**A development environment:**
- Node.js (v18+) or Flutter SDK installed
- Expo CLI or your preferred React Native setup
- A code editor — VS Code is the standard choice
- Git installed and a GitHub account ready

**Accounts you will likely need:**
- Firebase or Supabase (database + auth)
- Apple Developer account ($99/year — required for iOS)
- Google Play Console ($25 one-time — required for Android)
- A deployment platform account (Railway, Render, or similar) if you need a backend

You do not need all of these on day one. Each module will tell you exactly when you need what.

## A Word on Pace

Personal projects fail for one reason more than any other: they stall mid-build.

The feature that seemed small takes a week. The bug that should take an hour takes a day. Motivation dips. Other life happens. The project sits.

The antidote is momentum, not speed.

**Finish one module before starting the next.** Complete the checklist before you move on. A module that is 90% done is not done — and 90% done across five modules is a project that is nowhere.

Small, complete steps compound. Incomplete big steps don't.

## Your Curriculum

Here is what you are building toward:

**Phase 1 — Discovery & Design**
Define what you're building and for whom. Design the experience before writing a line of code.

**Phase 2 — Architecture**
Make the foundational decisions: stack, state, database, auth, offline strategy. These are the decisions that are hardest to change later.

**Phase 3 — Development**
Build it. Frontend, backend, permissions, notifications, testing.

**Phase 4 — Deployment**
Ship it. Security, performance, store listings, privacy.

**Phase 5 — Growth**
Learn from it. Analytics, user feedback, roadmap.

## One Question Before You Begin

You don't need to have the answer fully formed. But start here:

> **Who is one specific person this app would genuinely help — and what problem does it solve for them?**

Not a demographic. Not a user persona template. A real person, or someone real enough to be specific.

That person will guide every decision you make from here.

When you are ready — move to **Idea Definition.**
