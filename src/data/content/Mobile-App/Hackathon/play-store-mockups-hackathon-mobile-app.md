# Play Store Mockups

You are not submitting to the Play Store.

But Play Store and App Store mockups are one of the highest-leverage visual assets you can produce for a hackathon. They reframe your app from "a demo" to "a product that exists in the world."

Judges have seen hundreds of pitches with screen recordings and slide screenshots. A polished store listing visual stops them.

---

## What These Are Actually For

Play Store mockups serve three purposes in a hackathon context:

**1. Pitch deck slides** — A device frame with your app inside looks like a real product launch.

**2. Submission assets** — Many hackathons ask for screenshots or a project banner. A store mockup satisfies both.

**3. Perception shift** — It signals that your team thought about distribution, not just implementation.

You do not need to publish anything. You need the visuals.

---

## The Assets You Need

| Asset | Dimensions | Where It's Used |
|---|---|---|
| Feature Graphic | 1024 × 500px | Play Store banner, pitch deck header |
| Phone Screenshots | 1080 × 1920px (portrait) | Store listing, pitch deck slides |
| App Icon | 512 × 512px | Store listing, submission form |
| Framed Device Mockup | Custom | Pitch deck, project page |

For a hackathon: prioritise the Feature Graphic and 3–4 framed screenshots. These cover 90% of use cases.

## What Makes a Great Store Screenshot

Each screenshot communicates one thing. Not five things. One.

**The formula:**

```
[Headline — what this screen does]
        [Device frame with your actual screen]
[Optional: one-line supporting text]
```

**Screenshot sequence for a typical app (5 screens):**

| # | Screen | Headline |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Home / Feed | Your hook — the core value in one line |
| 2 | Core feature screen | The thing your app actually does |
| 3 | A native feature (camera, map, etc.) | The mobile-native moment |
| 4 | Profile or social proof | The personalisation layer |
| 5 | Onboarding or empty → full state | The "getting started" moment |

---

## Headline Writing

The headline above each screenshot is not a label. It is a sell.

**Weak:** "Home Screen"
**Strong:** "Everything you care about, in one place."

**Weak:** "Create a Post"
**Strong:** "Share in seconds. Your audience is waiting."

**Weak:** "Profile"
**Strong:** "Your story. Your way."

```
Copy Prompt ↓
```

> I'm building a mobile app called [App Name]. It does: [one-sentence description]. Write 5 Play Store screenshot headlines — one for each of these screens: [list your 5 screens]. Each headline should be punchy, benefit-focused, and under 8 words. Write in the tone of [modern / playful / professional / bold].

## The Feature Graphic

The Feature Graphic is the 1024 × 500px banner at the top of your Play Store listing.

It should contain:
- Your app name / wordmark
- Your one-line tagline
- A visual element (app screenshot, illustration, or abstract graphic)
- Your brand colors as the background

It should not contain:
- More than 10 words total
- Pricing information
- Device frames (the Play Store adds these separately)
- Low-contrast text

```
Copy Prompt ↓
```

> Design a Play Store Feature Graphic concept for my app [App Name]. Tagline: [your tagline]. Brand colors: [primary, secondary]. Visual style: [minimal / bold / gradient / dark / light]. Describe the layout, color usage, typography treatment, and what visual element to use. I will implement this in Figma / Canva.

## App Icon Polish

Your app icon appears at 512 × 512px in the store and shrinks to 48px on a home screen.

It must work at both sizes.

**Quick checklist for your icon:**
- [ ] No text (illegible at small sizes)
- [ ] Single, clear visual concept — not a collage
- [ ] Recognisable at 48px
- [ ] Rounded corners handled by the platform (export square, no border radius)
- [ ] Not a generic stock icon

If your icon is currently a default Expo/Flutter icon: fix this before any screenshot. It appears in every framed mockup.

```
Copy Prompt ↓
```

> I need an app icon concept for [App Name], a [one-line description] app. Design style: [minimal / bold / illustrative / abstract]. Brand colors: [list]. The icon should convey [one feeling or idea]. Describe the visual concept, shape, color usage, and how it scales to 48px. I will implement this in Figma or have it generated.

## Generating Mockup Screenshots

### Step 1 — Capture clean screenshots

From your device or simulator:
- Hide the status bar or use a clean status bar (9:41 AM, full battery, full signal)
- Navigate to the exact screen state you want
- Use the highest resolution available

**React Native (Expo):** Screenshots from iOS Simulator are automatically @3x resolution.
**Flutter:** Use the device simulator's screenshot function for consistent output.

### Step 2 — Drop into device frame

Upload to Previewed.app or Shots.so:
- Choose a device frame that matches your target platform
- Adjust the screenshot position within the frame
- Export at 1080 × 1920px or higher

### Step 3 — Add headline overlay

In Figma, Canva, or directly in the mockup tool:
- Add your headline above or below the device frame
- Use your brand font and colors
- Keep text large enough to read on a slide (minimum 36pt)

## Pitch Deck Integration

Once you have 3–4 framed screenshots and a Feature Graphic, use them like this:

| Slide | Asset |
|---|---|
| Hero / title slide | Feature Graphic as full-bleed background |
| Product walkthrough | One screenshot per slide, with headline |
| "How it works" | 3 screenshots side-by-side in one slide |
| Closing / CTA | Feature Graphic or app icon + tagline |

A pitch deck with real device mockups looks like a product launch. A pitch deck with raw screenshots looks like a demo.

## What Judges Actually Notice

You have approximately 3 seconds of attention when a judge looks at your project listing or submission page.

In those 3 seconds they see:
1. Your app icon
2. Your Feature Graphic / banner
3. Your first screenshot headline

If those three things are polished and coherent, they lean in.

If the icon is a placeholder, the banner is missing, and the screenshots are raw — they lean back.

Three assets. Three seconds. Make them count.
