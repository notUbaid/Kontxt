The feature graphic is a Google Play-specific asset that doesn't exist on iOS — a wide banner image shown at the top of your store listing and used in featured placements, category browsing, and some search contexts. It's smaller in scope than your full screenshot set, but it's prominent real estate worth getting right.

## What It's For

Unlike screenshots, which show app functionality, the feature graphic is closer to a billboard — brand identity and value proposition, often without showing UI at all. It's frequently what users see before they even tap into your full listing, especially in curated or featured placements.

> **Best Practice:** Don't just crop your app icon larger or paste in a screenshot. Design this as a distinct brand asset — think app store banner ad, not app interface.

## Technical Requirements

| Spec | Value |
|---|---|
| Dimensions | 1024×500px |
| Format | JPG or 24-bit PNG (no alpha) |
| File size | Under 1MB recommended |

This is a fixed aspect ratio (roughly 2:1, wide) — design for it directly rather than cropping a square or portrait asset into shape, which usually leaves awkward empty space or cuts off important content.

## What Works in This Format

- **App name and tagline**, large enough to read in a small thumbnail context — this graphic often displays quite small in browsing contexts before a user taps in
- **Brand colors and visual identity** consistent with your icon and screenshots, reinforcing recognition rather than introducing a new look
- **A single strong visual element** — abstract brand pattern, a stylized representation of your core feature, or a hero illustration — not a cluttered collage
- **High contrast text** that stays legible even when the graphic is scaled down significantly

## Common Mistakes

- Treating it as an afterthought and submitting a generic gradient with the app name slapped on
- Text too small or low-contrast to read when the graphic displays at reduced size in browsing UI
- Inconsistent branding versus the icon and screenshots, making the listing feel disjointed
- Showing detailed UI screenshots here instead of brand-level messaging — that's what the screenshot set is for
- Critical content placed near the edges, getting cropped or obscured in certain display contexts

## Using AI Here

```
Help me write tagline copy for a Google Play feature graphic.

App name: [name]
Core value proposition: [one sentence]
Brand personality: [e.g., playful, professional, minimal, energetic]

Suggest 5 short taglines (under 6 words) that work at small display sizes,
paired with the app name. Prioritize clarity and brand voice over cleverness —
this needs to read instantly, not be parsed.
```

As with other visual store assets, AI is useful for the copy and creative direction; the actual graphic should be built in a design tool (Figma or similar) at the exact 1024×500px spec, matching your established icon and screenshot branding.

> **Validation:** Preview the graphic at a significantly reduced size (roughly thumbnail scale, not full resolution) before finalizing — if the text or key visual element isn't legible at that size, it won't perform well in the browsing contexts where it's actually shown.

## Quick Reference: Feature Graphic Checklist

- [ ] Exact 1024×500px dimensions, correct format
- [ ] Legible at small/thumbnail display size, not just at full resolution
- [ ] Consistent branding with app icon and screenshot set
- [ ] No critical content near the edges
- [ ] Communicates brand and value proposition without relying on detailed UI
