# Success Metrics

Most personal stores fail silently. Not because they don't make sales — because the builder had no idea what success looked like until it was too late to change anything.

You need to define what winning means before you write a single line of code.

---

## Why This Matters Before Development

Metrics defined after launch are rationalizations. Metrics defined before launch are decisions.

When you know your success criteria upfront:

- You build features that move those numbers, not features that feel interesting
- You know when to stop adding and start shipping
- You know whether your store is working or just existing
- You can justify technical tradeoffs using real data goals

---

## The Personal Store Reality Check

> ⚠️ **Warning**
>
> Personal projects don't need enterprise KPI frameworks. A 20-metric dashboard is noise. Tracking 3–5 meaningful numbers is enough to know if your store is alive and growing.

Don't track vanity metrics. Track signal metrics.

| Vanity Metric | Signal Metric |
|---|---|
| Total page views | Unique visitors who reached product page |
| Total sessions | Sessions that added to cart |
| Followers | Returning customers |
| Orders created | Orders completed + paid |
| Email list size | Email open rate + click rate |

---

## The Metric Tiers

Pick metrics across these three tiers. You don't need all of them — you need the right ones for your store's current phase.

### Tier 1 — Survival Metrics

These tell you if the store is working at a fundamental level.

| Metric | What It Tells You | Healthy Range (Early Stage) |
|---|---|---|
| **Conversion Rate** | % of visitors who purchase | 1–3% |
| **Cart Abandonment Rate** | % who add to cart but don't buy | 60–80% is normal; above 85% is a problem |
| **Average Order Value (AOV)** | Revenue per completed order | Depends on your catalog |
| **Return Customer Rate** | % of buyers who come back | 20%+ is solid for personal stores |

### Tier 2 — Health Metrics

These tell you if your store has a future.

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **Email Capture Rate** | Are visitors opting into your list? |
| **Product Page Bounce Rate** | Are people reading your product pages? |
| **Search Usage Rate** | Are people searching, and finding things? |
| **Mobile vs Desktop Split** | Where to invest UI effort |

### Tier 3 — Growth Metrics

Track these once you're past launch.

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| **Month-over-Month Revenue Growth** | Is the store growing? |
| **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** | What does it cost to get one buyer? |
| **Lifetime Value (LTV)** | What is one customer worth long-term? |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | Is acquisition sustainable? |

## Define Your Personal Targets

Don't copy someone else's benchmarks. Define targets that are meaningful for your specific store, your catalog size, and your traffic sources.

Use this framework:

**Minimum Viable Success** — the floor. If you hit this, the store is working.

**Target** — what you're genuinely optimizing for over the next 90 days.

**Stretch Goal** — aspirational but grounded.

---

> 💡 **Tip: Start With One Conversion Rate Goal**
>
> If you track nothing else, track your store's overall conversion rate. It compresses the health of your product pages, checkout flow, pricing, and trust signals into a single number. If that number is moving up, you're improving. If it's stuck or falling, something is broken.

---

## ✅ Your Metrics Decision Checklist

Work through this before moving to the next phase.

- [ ] I have chosen 2–3 Tier 1 metrics to track from day one
- [ ] I have defined my Minimum Viable Success threshold for conversion rate
- [ ] I know what cart abandonment rate would trigger me to investigate the checkout flow
- [ ] I have decided what counts as a "successful" store for this project personally
- [ ] I have identified which analytics tool I will use to track these (Google Analytics 4, Plausible, or Fathom)
- [ ] I am not tracking more than 5 core metrics at launch

---

## Analytics Tool Decision

| Tool | Best For | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Google Analytics 4** | Free, powerful, good for e-commerce funnels | Free |
| **Plausible** | Simple, privacy-first, great UX | ~$9/mo |
| **Fathom** | Minimal, GDPR-compliant, fast setup | ~$14/mo |

For a personal store, GA4 is sufficient. Set up e-commerce tracking from day one — retrofitting it later is painful.

## AI Prompt — Define Your Success Metrics

Use this when your product catalog and target audience are roughly defined.

```
I am building a personal e-commerce store selling [product type] targeting [audience].

My store will have approximately [number] products at launch.

My primary traffic source will be [organic search / social / direct / paid].

Help me define:
1. The 3 most important metrics I should track for this specific store
2. Realistic benchmarks for each metric in the first 90 days
3. One leading indicator metric that would predict conversion rate problems early
4. The one metric I should check every week without fail

Keep the output concise. I want signal, not a dashboard.
```

> 📋 Copy this prompt, fill in your details, and paste into your AI tool of choice. Use the output to complete the checklist above.

## What Comes Next

Once your success metrics are defined, every decision in Phase 1 and beyond has a testable hypothesis attached to it.

When you're designing your checkout flow: *this decision should improve cart abandonment rate*.

When you're choosing a product page layout: *this should improve conversion rate from product views to add-to-cart*.

That's the shift from guessing to engineering.
