# Email Marketing Strategy

In a production e-commerce business, Email Marketing (and SMS) should account for 20% to 30% of your total revenue. 

Unlike Facebook or Google ads, you *own* your email list. It is a zero-CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) channel. However, if your engineering integration is brittle, automation flows will break, customers will receive the wrong emails, and your domain will be blacklisted for spam.

---

## 1. The Event-Driven Architecture (CDP Integration)

At scale, you do not manually upload CSV lists to an email provider. 

**The Implementation:**
You must implement an Event-Driven Customer Data Platform (CDP) approach.
- Use a specialized e-commerce marketing platform like **Klaviyo** or **Omnisend**.
- Your backend and frontend must fire strictly typed events to the CDP.
  - `Placed_Order` (Backend: Fires when Stripe captures payment).
  - `Fulfilled_Order` (Backend: Fires when the 3PL ships the item).
  - `Added_to_Cart` (Frontend: Fires when the user clicks the cart button).
  - `Viewed_Product` (Frontend: Fires when the PDP loads).
- The marketing team uses these real-time events to build automated visual flows (e.g., "If `Added_to_Cart` fires, but `Placed_Order` does NOT fire within 2 hours, send the Abandoned Cart email").

---

## 2. The Abandoned Cart Recovery

This is the highest ROI automated flow in e-commerce. ~70% of carts are abandoned.

**The Engineering Constraint:**
To send an abandoned cart email, you must know the user's email address *before* they complete checkout.
- **The Solution:** Implement early email capture. If a user is logged in, you know their email. If they are a guest, capture their email at the very first step of checkout, or via a pop-up offering a 10% discount, *before* they enter their shipping address.
- Sync this email to the CDP immediately, tagging it to their active cart session ID.
- **Deep Linking:** The email generated by Klaviyo must contain a dynamic link that rebuilds the user's exact cart in Redis and drops them directly back into the checkout flow, bypassing the store entirely.

---

## 3. Post-Purchase Flows (The LTV Driver)

The customer journey does not end at the "Thank You" page. It begins there.

**The Implementation:**
Trigger a sequence of emails based on the `Placed_Order` event.
- **Day 0:** Order Confirmation (Transactional).
- **Day 2:** Educational content ("How to care for your new leather boots").
- **Day 5:** Review Request (Triggered *only* if the order status is `DELIVERED`).
- **Day 14:** Cross-Sell Push ("Complete the look with this matching belt").
- **Day 60:** Win-back campaign (If no second purchase has been made).

---

## 4. Deliverability and Compliance (CAN-SPAM / GDPR)

If you ignore deliverability engineering, your emails will go to the Gmail Spam folder, and your revenue will plummet.

**The Implementation:**
- **DNS Records:** You must authenticate your sending domain by configuring SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records in your DNS provider (e.g., Cloudflare).
- **Unsubscribe Links:** Every marketing email must have a one-click unsubscribe link. Your CDP handles this, but your backend must respect the sync. If a user clicks unsubscribe, Klaviyo will flag them as suppressed. You must never attempt to send them a marketing email via a custom backend script again.
- **Transactional vs Marketing:** Keep them separate. Order receipts (Transactional) can be sent to users who have opted out of marketing. Promotional blasts (Marketing) cannot.

## Email Marketing Checklist

- [ ] Event-Driven CDP integration (Klaviyo/Omnisend) configured for real-time frontend and backend event syncing
- [ ] Early email capture implemented in the checkout flow to maximize Abandoned Cart recovery reach
- [ ] Cart Rebuild URLs engineered to allow seamless return-to-checkout from email links
- [ ] Post-purchase flow architecture designed (Education, Review Requests, Cross-sells)
- [ ] SPF, DKIM, and DMARC DNS records verified for maximum deliverability
- [ ] Strict technical separation maintained between Transactional (Receipts) and Marketing (Promotions) email pipelines
